About Me

My passion for computer science lies largely in using it to create programs that allow people to take advantage of our greatest resource: each other. I admire modern day companies that use technology to bring people together for mutual benefit. Developing and making these sorts of technologies easy to use and accessible to everyone is a goal I have for myself moving forward.

My Personal Projects

Tix

tixconcerts.com
My Code

During the summer after my freshman year of college, my friends and I were looking for concerts in the Boston area. I found it extremely frustrating that there was no way to have a "favorite artists" list on ticketmaster or stubhub. This was the beginnings of my idea for Tix.

Using Spotify and Ticketmaster's API, Tix allows you to conveniently view all of the concerts for your top artist on Spotify. I developed Tix completely on my own, and it helped me gain a tremendous amount of knowledge and experience using API's and developing a website in general. As of right now, 35 new people use Tix each day, and this number is still growing as I continue to develop its functionality.

My Code

As the final project to my introductory computer science class, I had to choose from a variety of games to make from scrath, and I chose Pacman. Built in Java, using object-oriented programming, I created Pacman completely on my own, without any stencil code. Experience

Black Duck Software

Black Duck Software is an open source risk management firm with three hundred full time employees and nearly eighty million dollars in annual revenue. By scanning clients’ code, Black Duck identifies every open-source components used in a company's software. Utilizing its deep knowledge base, Black Duck identifies the licenses and security risks associated with each open-source component, and then evaluates the danger for the company in having those open-source components in their code. Black Duck's notable partners include Google, HP, and RedHat, and a few of its notable customers include Samsung, Intel, NASA, Uber, Verizon, BP, and Reddit.

The summer after my freshman year of college, I worked at Black Duck as a Sales Engineer Intern. As a Sales Engineer Intern, I worked closely with both the software engineering and business teams. A majority of my time was spent working alongside the integrations team to develop new functionality for our products. I spent several weeks developing a program in Java that would identify open-source dependencies in projects written in R, a language used mostly for data science. This program was then implemented into he company's main product: Black Duck Hub-Detect. In addition to programming, I was also able to experience the business side of technology and understand better the nuance of how deals are made with customers in the tech industry. Specifically, providing proof of concepts and demos for customers helped me gain a deep knowledge of Black Duck’s product and how to implement it.
